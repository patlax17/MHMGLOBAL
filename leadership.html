Background

Dr. Ikenna Achebe is a physician, epidemiologist, and bioethicist dedicated to advancing mental health equity for underserved populations. His work bridges clinical psychiatry, public health research, and community-based healing practices.

As Senior Program Associate for Project Opioid Court REACH at Columbia University and the New York State Psychiatric Institute, Dr. Achebe leads initiatives addressing substance use disorders and mental health in justice-involved populations.

Research & Community Leadership

Dr. Achebe's research focuses on the intersection of community violence, trauma, and mental health outcomes in urban communities. His epidemiological work examines social determinants of psychological well-being and the effectiveness of community-led interventions.

Through his training in urban bioethics, Dr. Achebe brings critical attention to questions of justice, equity, and power in mental health systems. He has developed innovative models that center community voices in program design and evaluation.

Approach

Dr. Achebe's leadership philosophy centers on the belief that sustainable mental health equity requires both clinical rigor and deep community partnership. He advocates for healing justice frameworks that address root causes of trauma while honoring diverse cultural healing traditions.

Under his direction, Mental Health Matters Global has developed evidence-based programs that are implemented in partnership with communities, ensuring interventions are both scientifically sound and culturally responsive.
