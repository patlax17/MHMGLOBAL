SOS Project: Responding to Community Violence

The SOS Project addresses community violence as a public health crisis, providing immediate de-escalation support and long-term trauma recovery through community-led interventions.

The Context

Community violence creates cascading mental health impacts that extend far beyond direct victims. Witnesses, families, and entire neighborhoods experience trauma that often goes unaddressed by traditional mental health systems.

The SOS Project recognizes that communities most impacted by violence hold essential wisdom about healing and safety. Our model trains community members as first responders who can provide culturally grounded support in moments of crisis.

How It Works

1

De-escalation Training

Community members receive evidence-based training in conflict de-escalation, trauma-informed communication, and crisis response. Trained "SOS Envoys" become trusted resources within their neighborhoods.

2

Healing Circles

Following violent incidents, SOS Envoys facilitate healing circles that bring together those impacted. These peer-led spaces provide immediate emotional support and connection in the aftermath of trauma.

3

Long-term Support

Participants are connected to ongoing mental health resources, including clinical services, peer support groups, and community wellness programs. We track outcomes and provide sustained follow-up.

World Mental Health Initiative (WMHI)

WMHI advances mental health equity for global diaspora communities through culturally responsive programs and international partnerships.

Our Global Approach

Mental health challenges are shaped by cultural context, migration experiences, and systemic barriers that vary across communities. WMHI develops programs tailored to the specific needs of diaspora populations, honoring diverse healing traditions while providing evidence-based support.

We partner with community organizations, faith leaders, and cultural institutions to ensure our programs are accessible, trusted, and effective.

Community Healing Circles

Peer-led spaces for collective healing, mutual support, and community resilience.

The Peer-Led Model

Community Healing Circles are facilitated by trained peers who have lived experience with mental health challenges. This model recognizes that healing happens in community and that those who have walked similar paths are uniquely positioned to offer support.

Circles provide a confidential, non-judgmental space for participants to share their experiences, learn coping strategies, and build connections with others who understand their journey.

Circle Topics

Grief & Loss

Processing loss and finding pathways forward together.

Anxiety & Depression

Sharing strategies for managing symptoms and building resilience.

Trauma Recovery

Healing from traumatic experiences in community.

Caregiver Support

Mutual support for those caring for loved ones.
